1. DESCRIPTION OF THE CONTEST:

The GlassFish Awards Program (the "Contest") is designed to encourage and recognize innovation and community participation with GlassFish Community related activities and contributions. An Entry may consist of either: (1) a report to address known bugs in a GlassFish project (a "Bug Report Entry"); or (2) an original contribution to or benefit of an existing or new GlassFish project (a "Contribution Entry"). Prize winners will be selected by a panel of judges based on the criteria set forth in these Official Rules.

2. ELIGIBILITY:

This Contest is open only to individuals who lawfully reside in the following countries and who have reached the legal age of majority: Argentina, Australia, Austria, Belgium, Brazil, Bulgaria, Cambodia, Canada, Chile, China, Croatia, Czech Republic, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Hungary, India, Indonesia, Ireland, Israel, Italy, Japan, Korea, Malaysia, Mexico, New Zealand, Norway, Peru, Poland, Romania, Russia, Singapore, South Africa, Spain, Sri Lanka, Sweden, Switzerland, Taiwan, Thailand, the Netherlands, the Philippines, the United Kingdom, Turkey, Venezuela, and the fifty United States and the District of Columbia. Void in Puerto Rico, Province of Quebec, Canada and where prohibited by law. Void in Puerto Rico, Province of Quebec, Canada and where prohibited by law. Individuals or teams of up to three (3) individuals are eligible. Companies are not eligible to enter. Employees of Sponsor and Sun Microsystems, Inc. ("Sun"), and each of their respective parent companies, affiliates and subsidiaries, participating advertising and promotion agencies (and members of their immediate family, defined as parents, children, siblings and spouse, regardless of where they reside, and/or those living in the same household) are not eligible. You must also have access to the Internet and a valid email address in order to enter. Entries may be submitted by a single individual or by a team of up to three (3) individuals. There is no limit on number of entries per person or team. However, one individual may not be awarded more than five (5) prizes in the Bug Report category or more than one (1) prize in the Contribution category. If an Entry is submitted by a team, each individual member of the team must meet the eligibility requirements set forth herein. The term "Entrant" as used in these Official Rules refers to each eligible individual and/or team that submits an entry.

IMPORTANT NOTICE TO ENTRANTS: ALL DEADLINES SET FORTH IN THESE OFFICIAL RULES ARE STATED IN UNITED STATES PACIFIC STANDARD TIME. ENTRANTS ARE RESPONSIBLE FOR DETERMINING THE CORRESPONDING TIME ZONE IN THEIR RESPECTIVE JURISDICTIONS.

3. HOW TO ENTER:

To enter, you must be a member of the GlassFish Community. If you are not a member of the GlassFish Community, go to http://www.java.net/servlets/Join to register. If your Entry contains a contribution to or is a derivation of a GlassFish Community Project, it must be contributed to the GlassFish project under the terms of the Sun Contributor Agreement (see http://www.sun.com/software/opensource/sca.pdf). Legal requirements of specific GlassFish projects are available at https://glassfish.java.net/public/GovernancePolicy.html. All Entries must be submitted in accordance with an applicable OSI approved license, available at http://www.opensource.org/licenses.

For team Entries, each individual member of the team must be a member of the GlassFish Community and (if applicable) must have signed the Sun Contributor Agreement at the time the Entry is submitted. Failure to do so will result in the disqualification the team's Entry. Also, the team must designate one individual member of the team as the Team Leader for contact purposes only. The team leader shall be responsible for submitting the Entry on behalf of the team and shall designate all members of the team with the Entry submission.

To enter, you must develop and submit either a Bug Report Entry or a Contribution Entry (each referred to as the "Entry") that meets the goals set forth below and complies with the Submission Guidelines and other requirements of these Official Rules. The Bug Report Entry and Contribution Entry is described below:

A. Bug Report Entry: Submit a report that addresses known bugs in a GlassFish project. Entry must comply with the bug tracking system guidelines for the GlassFish project, available at http://glassfish.org/issues. To report bugs, you will be required to log in to the glassfish.org infrastructure.

B. Contribution Entry: Submit an original contribution which helps to enhance or develop the GlassFish Community. Examples of contributions are as follows: Develop a software application that enhances or augments the value of GlassFish developments; (ii) Create blueprints or documentation on specific GlassFish topics, such as clustering, horizontal scaling techniques, architecture tuning, scripting); (iii) Develop curricula or tools courseware that are localized and technology focused; (iv) Implement a compatible, free software alternative to a remaining bit of encumbered code; (v) Set up user-groups; (vi) Create localized portals; (vii) Establish university presence; or (viii) Conduct GlassFish research, such as leveraging Solaris DTrace with GlassFish to improve troubleshooting.

In addition, the Entry must meet each of the following requirements:

A. The Entry must be written in plain text and in English. Although the Entry must be written in English, the implementation of the Entry need not be in English. For example, an Entrant may submit a Entry written in English demonstrating the localization of a GlassFish portal in Mandarin.

B. The Entry must be an original idea that can be employed in the GlassFish Community with the potential to leverage and extend the code base or Community infrastructure and features.

C. The Entry must be carried out in accordance with any and all legal requirements of the specific GlassFish project that is the subject of the Entry. For example, if an Entry proposes to add a new module to the GlassFish Application Server, it must be contributed to the GlassFish project under the terms of the Sun Contributor Agreement (see http://www.sun.com/software/opensource/sca.pdf). Legal requirements of specific GlassFish projects are available at https://glassfish.java.net/public/GovernancePolicy.html.

D. The Entry must not exceed a maximum of 1000 words.

E. To submit your Entry, e-mail your Entry to gap-submissions@glassfish.java.net. All Entries must be received between January 29, 2008 at 12:00 A.M. (PST) and June 30, 2008 at 11:59 P.M. (PST). The Sponsor's clock is the official time clock of the Contest. Proof of submission does not constitute proof of receipt.

F. The Entry must also include the individual and each team member's full name, email address, physical address, and country of residence.

4. SUBMISSION GUIDELINES:

In addition to the requirements set forth above, each Entry must comply with the following Submission Guidelines:

A. The Entry may not contain any content or material that is lewd, obscene, sexually explicit, pornographic, disparaging, defamatory, libelous, obscene, or otherwise inappropriate or objectionable, as determined by the Judges and/or Sponsor in their sole and absolute discretion.

B. The Entry must be the Entrant's original work, created solely by the Entrant, and must not infringe the copyright, trademark, privacy, publicity, or other intellectual rights of any person or entity.

C. All Entries, including without limitation, code, documentation and any elements of Entry must be available under at least one OSI approved open source license, available at http://www.opensource.org/licenses.

D. The Entry must not contain any personally identifiable information of any person other than the Entrant. Should the Entrant include personally identifiable information about him/herself in his/her Entry, Entrant acknowledges and agrees that such information will be disclosed publicly and Entrant is solely responsible for any consequences thereof.

E. The Entry must not contain any commercial content or logos of any entity other than Sponsor and Sun Microsystems, Inc. which may be incorporated only under the terms of any applicable licenses.

The Judges and/or Sponsor reserve the right to disqualify at any time, any Entry that they determine, in their sole and absolute discretion does not meet the requirements of the Official Rules, the Sun Contributor Agreement (if applicable), the applicable OSI license or these Submission Guidelines.

5. JUDGING/WINNER SELECTION:

A. Bug Report Entries will be judged by a panel based on the following equally weighted judging criteria:

  1. Impact of fix
  2. Difficulty of fix
  3. Completeness of fix
  4. Clarity of test case for bug report
  5. Description of bug fix
  6. Usefulness of fix

B. Contribution Entries will be judged by a panel based on the following equally weighted judging criteria:

  1. Originality
  2. Quality
  3. Usefulness to the GlassFish Community
  4. Completeness
  5. Impact of the Entry on the goals of the GlassFish Community, including: Enabling component reuse; (ii) Making the Java EE 5 Reference Implementation widely available; and (iii) Increasing the ease by which developers can participate in the GlassFish Community.

In the event of a tie, the person among the tied Entries with the highest score in Usefulness will be declared the winner. If ties still remain the tied entries will be re-judged on the same criteria. The Judges/Sponsor reserve the right to terminate the Contest and not to award any Prizes, if in their sole and absolute discretion they do not receive a sufficient number of eligible or qualified entries for the Contest. In the event that more than one Entry is received with the same or nearly identical idea, only the first such Entry received will be eligible, based on the date of the first draft submission of the Entry to the Contest entry mailing list. Winners will be notified by email, telephone or postal mail, within Sponsor's discretion. Decisions of Judges are final and binding.

6. PRIZES:

Type Total Prize Package Division of Prize Package
Bug Report $50,000 Divided equally among winners
Contribution $120,000 (1) Grand Prize: $20,000, (1) Second Prize: $10,000, (1) Third Prize: $10,000, (20) Base Prizes: 4,000 each

The Judges will also award Honorable Mention prizes to Entries from among both the Bug Report Entries and the Contribution Entries based on the judging criteria set forth above. $5,000 will be divided evenly among the winning Honorable Mention Awards. The number of Honorable Mention awards is within the sole discretion of the Judges. Sponsor reserves the right not to award any Honorable Mention awards if, in its sole and absolute discretion an inadequate number of qualified winning entries are received.

One individual may not be awarded more than five (5) prizes in the Bug Report category or more than one (1) prize in the Contribution category. An Honorable Mention award is considered to be part of both the Bug Report category and the Contribution category.

All other expenses not specified herein are the responsibility of the winners. All costs associated with currency exchange are the sole responsibility of the winners. ALL TAXES ARE THE SOLE RESPONSIBILITY OF THE WINNERS. In the event that a winning Entry was submitted by a team, the Prize will be divided equally among all team members who were named on the Entry submission.

Projects completed as a part of the Contest will be treated the same as any other contributions to the GlassFish Project, and subject to the governance processes that control acceptance and integration of contributions, available at https://glassfish.java.net/public/GovernancePolicy.html.
